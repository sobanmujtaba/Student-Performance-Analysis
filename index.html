Correlation Findings

+0.92
Previous Scores
Dominant predictor by a large margin. Current performance is largely a continuation of prior trajectory. This shifts curriculum design priority toward pre-entry diagnostics and early-term interventions rather than in-semester behaviour change.
+0.37
Hours Studied
Moderate positive effect. The only meaningfully modifiable behavioural variable in this dataset. However, Cluster 1 shows that hours alone are insufficient without addressing underlying knowledge gaps.
+0.05
Sleep Hours
Negligible linear relationship with Performance Index. Sleep hours are nearly identical across all four clusters (6.4 to 6.6h), which may explain the weak correlation — there is no spread to detect a relationship from.
+0.04
Practice Papers
Negligible linear effect. This does not mean practice is unimportant. Its effect may be non-linear or may already be captured through previous scores.
+0.02
Extracurricular Activities
No detectable linear association. Participation is uniform at 49-51% across all four clusters, making it impossible for this feature to differentiate performance in this dataset.
// 05

Method

Feature Engineering

Extracurricular Activities encoded as binary integer (1=Yes, 0=No). K-Means requires numeric input and has no built-in handling for categorical variables.

Scaling

StandardScaler applied before clustering. Without scaling, Previous Scores (range ~60) would dominate over Sleep Hours (range ~5) purely due to magnitude difference in Euclidean distance.

Choosing K

Inertia and silhouette score computed for k=2 to 8. Scores are modest across all k (continuous dataset, no hard-edged blobs). k=4 chosen for pedagogical interpretability.

Clustering

KMeans(n_clusters=4, n_init=10). Clusters relabelled in ascending order of mean Performance Index after fitting. PCA used for 2D scatter only.

Correlation

Pearson r between each feature and Performance Index. With n=10,000, all p-values are effectively zero, so effect magnitude is the relevant metric, not significance alone.

Stack

Python 3.10, pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn, Matplotlib, SciPy. This page rendered with Chart.js, no build step required.
